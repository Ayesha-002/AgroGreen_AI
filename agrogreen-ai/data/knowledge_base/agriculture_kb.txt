PAKISTAN AGRICULTURE KNOWLEDGE BASE
=====================================

## WHEAT FARMING IN PAKISTAN

Wheat is the most important food crop in Pakistan, grown on about 9 million hectares.
Best growing regions: Punjab, Sindh, KPK.
Sowing time: November to December.
Harvesting time: April to May.
Recommended varieties: Punjab-2011, Faisalabad-2008, Sehar-2006, Galaxy-2013.
Water requirement: 4-5 irrigations during growing season.
Fertilizer: Apply 120 kg/ha Nitrogen, 90 kg/ha Phosphorus, 60 kg/ha Potassium.
Common diseases: Yellow rust, stem rust, leaf blight.
Treatment for rust: Spray Tilt 250 EC or Bayleton fungicide.
Yield: 3-4 tonnes per hectare under good management.

## RICE FARMING IN SINDH

Rice is widely cultivated in Sindh province, especially in Larkana, Sukkur, Ghotki.
Best varieties: Irri-6, Irri-9, Super Basmati, Shaheen Basmati.
Transplanting time: June to July.
Harvesting: October to November.
Water requirement: 1200-1500 mm throughout season.
Fertilizer: 120-150 kg/ha Urea, 60-75 kg/ha DAP.
Common pest: Brown plant hopper, stem borer, blast disease.
Treatment for blast: Spray Beam 75WP or Fujione fungicide.

## TOMATO CULTIVATION

Tomatoes are grown throughout Pakistan year-round in different climates.
Spring crop: February-March sowing.
Autumn crop: August-September sowing.
Best varieties: Roma, Nagina, Riogrande, Hybrid varieties.
Soil requirement: Well-drained loamy soil, pH 6.0-7.0.
Fertilizer: 200 kg/ha Nitrogen, 150 kg/ha Phosphorus, 100 kg/ha Potassium.
Irrigation: Drip irrigation recommended. Water every 2-3 days.
Common diseases: Early blight, late blight, bacterial wilt.
Yellow leaves cause: Nitrogen deficiency, overwatering, or Fusarium wilt.
Treatment for yellow leaves: Apply balanced NPK fertilizer, check drainage.
Treatment for early blight: Spray Mancozeb or Dithane M-45.

## COTTON FARMING

Cotton is a major cash crop grown in Punjab and Sindh.
Sowing time: April to May.
Harvesting: October to December.
Varieties: CIM-496, CIM-573, BH-167, MNH-886.
Water requirement: 7-8 irrigations.
Fertilizer: 150 kg/ha Nitrogen, 75 kg/ha Phosphorus.
Common pests: Whitefly, bollworm, jassid, thrips.
Whitefly treatment: Spray Imidacloprid or Confidor.
Bollworm treatment: Spray Cypermethrin or Karate.

## SUGARCANE FARMING

Sugarcane is grown mainly in Punjab and Sindh.
Planting time: February-March (spring) or October-November (autumn).
Varieties: CP77-400, HSF-240, SPF-234.
Water requirement: 10-12 irrigations.
Fertilizer: 250 kg/ha Nitrogen, 120 kg/ha Phosphorus.
Ratoon crop: Can be taken 2-3 times after first harvest.

## SOIL HEALTH AND FERTILIZERS

Soil testing is important before applying fertilizers.
Sandy soil: Needs more organic matter. Apply farmyard manure 10-15 tonnes/ha.
Clay soil: Needs drainage improvement. Use green manure crops.
Alkaline soil: Treat with gypsum 300-500 kg/ha.
Saline soil: Leach with fresh water, use salt-tolerant varieties.
Organic matter: Apply compost or green manure every season.
Urea (46% N): Best for top dressing.
DAP (18% N, 46% P): Best for basal application.
SOP (50% K): Best for potassium requirement.

## PEST MANAGEMENT (IPM)

Integrated Pest Management reduces chemical use.
1. Monitor fields regularly (twice a week).
2. Use sticky traps for insects.
3. Encourage natural enemies (ladybirds, lacewings).
4. Use pheromone traps for bollworm.
5. Apply pesticides only when threshold is crossed.
6. Rotate crops to break pest cycles.
7. Use resistant varieties.

## IRRIGATION TECHNIQUES

Flood irrigation: Traditional, water wasteful (60-70% efficiency).
Furrow irrigation: Better than flood, suitable for row crops.
Sprinkler irrigation: 75-80% efficiency, good for wheat and vegetables.
Drip irrigation: 90-95% efficiency, best for vegetables and orchards.
Water saving tip: Irrigate in early morning or evening to reduce evaporation.

## CLIMATE CHANGE ADAPTATION

Climate change is affecting Pakistan agriculture:
- Rising temperatures reduce wheat yield.
- Irregular rainfall affects kharif crops.
- Glacier melt causing floods in northern areas.

Adaptation strategies:
- Grow heat-tolerant crop varieties.
- Use mulching to conserve soil moisture.
- Plant trees as windbreaks.
- Use solar-powered drip irrigation.
- Diversify crops to reduce risk.
- Practice rainwater harvesting.

## PLANT DISEASES — DIAGNOSIS AND TREATMENT

Yellow leaves (Chlorosis):
- Cause: Nitrogen deficiency, waterlogging, iron deficiency.
- Treatment: Apply Urea 2% foliar spray, improve drainage.

Brown spots on leaves:
- Cause: Fungal disease (Alternaria, Cercospora).
- Treatment: Spray Mancozeb 2.5 g/L water.

White powder on leaves (Powdery mildew):
- Treatment: Spray Sulfur-based fungicide or Carbendazim.

Wilting plants:
- Cause: Root rot, Fusarium wilt, water stress.
- Treatment: Drench with Captan or Carbendazim. Check irrigation.

Leaf curling:
- Cause: Virus transmitted by whitefly or mites.
- Treatment: Control whitefly with Imidacloprid.

## URBAN FARMING AND ROOFTOP GARDENS

Rooftop farming is growing in Pakistani cities.
Suitable crops: Tomatoes, spinach, coriander, mint, peppers, herbs.
Container sizes: 10-20 liter pots for vegetables.
Growing medium: Mix of soil, compost, and coco peat (1:1:1).
Irrigation: Drip irrigation or hand watering once/twice daily.
Sunlight: Minimum 6 hours direct sunlight needed.
Benefits: Fresh food, reduced heat island effect, mental health.

## LIVESTOCK INTEGRATION

Mixed farming with livestock improves income:
- Poultry: Eggs and meat, manure for crops.
- Dairy: Milk income, manure for biogas and fertilizer.
- Goats: Hardy animals, suit small farmers.
Feed crops: Berseem, sorghum, maize fodder.

## MARKET AND ECONOMICS

Best time to sell:
- Wheat: Store for 2-3 months after harvest when prices rise.
- Tomato: Sell immediately, perishable.
- Cotton: Hold for 1-2 months for better price.
- Rice: Sell after drying and storage.

Value addition:
- Tomato paste, pickles add value.
- Rice milling on-farm increases profit.
- Cotton ginning cooperatives help small farmers.
